Day-by-day

Master itinerary

8 Dec

Bangkok arrival

Easy start: hotel, massage, Jodd Fairs or Chinatown, then rooftop drinks.

  • Mahanakhon SkyWalk or Tichuca Rooftop
  • Street food night
  • No heavy sightseeing yet
9 Dec

Bangkok classic day

The big Bangkok day: temples, river, malls and a proper skyline evening.

  • Grand Palace + Wat Pho
  • Chao Phraya river boat
  • IconSiam / MBK / Siam Paragon
  • Mahanakhon at sunset
10 Dec

Transfer to Hua Hin

Morning in Bangkok if needed, then 3–4 h transfer to Hua Hin.

  • Check-in, beach walk, pool
  • Hua Hin Night Market
  • Early chill mode
11 Dec

Golf day

One relaxed golf round. Budget pick: Royal Hua Hin / Palm Hills. Premium pick: Pineapple Valley / Black Mountain.

  • Morning tee time
  • Caddie experience
  • Seafood dinner after round
12 Dec

Nature day: Phraya Nakhon Cave

The best Hua Hin area adventure. Go early, bring water, expect heat.

  • Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park
  • Phraya Nakhon Cave
  • Sunlight pavilion photo moment
13 Dec

Beach + markets

Slow day: beach, pool, massage and night markets.

  • Hua Hin Beach
  • Khao Takiab monkey mountain
  • Cicada / Tamarind Market
14 Dec

Final Hua Hin day

No stress. Enjoy the resort day and prepare for the early airport transfer.

  • Pool, massage, last Thai dinner
  • Pack bags in the evening
  • Sleep early
15 Dec

Hua Hin → BKK airport → Hong Kong

Very early departure from Hua Hin. Keep this as a pure travel day.

  • Big safety margin to BKK airport
  • Hong Kong arrival evening
  • Victoria Harbour + Temple Street
16 Dec

Hong Kong icons

Peak, Central, Star Ferry and skyline. This is the Hong Kong core day.

  • Victoria Peak + Peak Tram
  • Mid-Levels Escalator
  • Star Ferry at sunset
  • Tsim Sha Tsui waterfront
17 Dec

Macao day trip

Old Portuguese streets by day, Cotai casino lights by night.

  • Ruins of St. Paul’s
  • Senado Square
  • Portuguese egg tarts
  • Venetian / Cotai Strip
18 Dec

Lantau Island or city day

Choose nature and cable car, or keep it urban with markets and food.

  • Ngong Ping 360 + Big Buddha
  • Tai O fishing village
  • Alternative: Mong Kok + Sham Shui Po
19 Dec

Flexible day: Shenzhen option

Shenzhen if entry logistics are easy. Otherwise, do the best remaining Hong Kong day.

  • Option A: Shenzhen electronics / city vibe
  • Option B: Dragon’s Back hike + Stanley
  • Last proper rooftop / skyline night
20 Dec

Departure day

Flight at 17:40. Morning still usable, but no risky long trips.

  • Dim sum breakfast
  • Last shopping
  • Airport around 13:30–14:00

Food & drinks

Best food zones by city

Bangkok

Local: Chinatown, Jodd Fairs, Thipsamai, Polo Fried Chicken

Western/easy: The Commons, Sarnies, CentralWorld area

Rooftops: Mahanakhon, Tichuca, Octave, Vertigo

Hua Hin

Local: Night Market seafood, Tamarind Market, Cicada Market

Chill: beach restaurants, hotel breakfast, pool food

Best move: seafood dinner after golf

Hong Kong

Local: dim sum, wonton noodles, roast goose, egg waffles

Areas: Central, Tsim Sha Tsui, Mong Kok, Sham Shui Po

Views: TST waterfront, rooftop bars, Peak

Practical notes

Rules for a smooth trip

Do not overpack Bangkok

Two full days is enough for highlights. You have Hong Kong later for another mega-city hit.

Keep 15 Dec clean

Hua Hin to BKK airport needs a big margin. Treat it as travel only.

Macao is must-do

It gives the trip a totally different vibe and is more worth it than forcing Shenzhen.

One golf day is perfect

Enough to experience Hua Hin golf without making the trip too expensive.
